# BUILD · Pricing

> The Wadsworth run-of-show keeps pricing verbal in the room on purpose. This is the number sheet behind that conversation: what to say, what it costs, and why it holds up against what schools already pay. Prepared Jul 21, 2026.

## The one-line model

A flat annual site license for the whole school: unlimited athletes, unlimited coaches, every sport. Optional paid season pilot first, credited in full toward year one. No per-athlete tiers, no per-coach seats, no app-store anything.

## The numbers

| Offer | Price | What it covers |
|---|---|---|
| **Season pilot** | **$750 flat** | One group (a team or an off-season lifting group), one training cycle (~a season). Roster and first cycle set up together. 100% credited toward year one if they continue. |
| **Founding annual license** | **$1,500 / year** | The whole school: unlimited athletes, unlimited coaches, every sport. White-labeled to the school (colors, mascot, home-screen icon). Locked at $1,500 for as long as they stay continuous. |
| **Standard annual license** | **$2,000 / year** | Same as above, the rate after the founding cohort closes. |
| **2-year** | **$2,600** ($1,300/yr) | Founding rate, paid up front. |
| **3-year** | **$3,600** ($1,200/yr) | Founding rate, paid up front. |

Founding lever: for the very first school or two, you can drop the pilot to **$500** and call it a founding-partner rate. Keep it paid. A free pilot tells you nothing and de-anchors the license.

## Why these numbers hold

- **TeamBuildr** charges $900 to $2,800/year per school (up to 1,000 athletes, unlimited coaches), or $90 to $280/month. BUILD's $1,500 sits mid-band, so the buyer is not paying more than they would for the incumbent.
- **TrueCoach** is $26 to $137/month priced per client, which balloons for a full roster and was built for personal trainers, not teams.
- At parity price, BUILD wins on three things the incumbents do not do: the app is the school's brand on the kid's phone (not a vendor's), it installs from the browser with no app store, and it was built ten minutes away, so support is a person they can reach.

## What is included

Everything in the live product: roster with "gone quiet" flags, bulk programming and AI workout generation, athlete Train view with the rest timer and logging, the team Numbers board, coach to athlete messaging, and the white-label skinner. Unlimited athletes and coaches. All sports under one license.

## What is not included (yet, name it honestly if asked)

- No SSO / roster sync with school information systems. Rosters are added in-app or bulk-loaded.
- No native app-store app. It is an installable web app (this is a feature for schools, no IT app-store review, but say it plainly).
- Support is founder-direct, not a 24/7 help desk. For a local pilot that is a selling point; do not oversell it as an enterprise SLA.

## In the room (verbal script)

> "Pricing is simple. It is a flat license for the whole school, not per kid and not per coach, so it does not punish you for adding athletes. Every sport, unlimited rosters, fifteen hundred a year, and that is locked in as a founding school. To start, we run one group for a season for seven fifty, set up the roster and the first cycle together, and that seven fifty comes right off year one. So the real question this season is just: which group do we start with."

Then stop talking. The close is picking the group, not defending the price.

## Objection handling

- **"We already use [TeamBuildr / a spreadsheet]."** "Then you already know the workflow. This is the same job for the same money, except the app on the kids' phones is Wadsworth, not a vendor, and I am ten minutes away when you need something changed."
- **"Can we just try it free?"** "The pilot is the try. It is a season with a real group for seven fifty, and every dollar comes off year one, so if you continue it cost you nothing extra. Free pilots never get set up right because nobody owns them."
- **"That seems low / what is the catch."** "No catch. It is a founding rate. I am trading a lower price now for schools who will vouch for it later. That rate stays yours as long as you stay."
- **"Who else uses it?"** Be honest about the count. Founding-school framing turns 'you'd be first' into a feature, not a risk.
